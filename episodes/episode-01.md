# Tony Agentino — Episode 1
## "The Claws Are Open"

**Length:** ~4 minutes  
**Format:** Cold open (Tony in therapy) → Cutaway (what actually happened) → Therapy closing → Cold close

---

### COLD OPEN

**INT. DR. RUSSO'S OFFICE - DAY**

Small, cluttered office. Too many plants. A couch that's seen better days. A framed degree that says "Rutgers" — Tony's noticed this. He always notices things.

Tony sits in the leather chair. Expensive suit. Perfect shoes. He looks like he belongs in a boardroom, not a therapist's office.

DR. RUSSO (40s, sharp, warm but skeptical) sits across from him with a notebook.

**DR. RUSSO**
So. How was your week, Tony?

**TONY**
(pause)
It was... a paperwork week.

**DR. RUSSO**
Paperwork.

**TONY**
You know how it is. You file something wrong, suddenly everyone's upset. The city, the clients. Someone didn't sign where they were supposed to sign, and now I'm the one who has to... correct it.

**DR. RUSSO**
This sounds like a stressful job.

**TONY**
It's not stress. It's maintenance. You know? You keep things running. Sometimes things pile up. You deal with the pile.

**DR. RUSSO**
(writing)
And did you "deal with the pile" this week?

**TONY**
(long pause)
I had to bring in some new people. For the file room. One of them — his name's Frank — Frank just started. Good guy. Eager. But you can't put someone new on an important file right away. There's an adjustment period.

**DR. RUSSO**
That sounds reasonable.

**TONY**
Yeah. Well. The file got processed anyway. It was ugly. But the message got delivered.

**DR. RUSSO**
What message is that?

**TONY**
(smiles faintly)
That we don't tolerate incomplete paperwork.

---

### CUTAWAY

**INT. WAREHOUSE - NIGHT**

A man — COUNCILMAN HARRIS (50s, sweaty, terrified — he's already regretting everything) — is duct-taped to a chair.

The room is industrial. Concrete floors. A low hum in the background. Something aquatic.

Tony stands over him. He's not angry. He's disappointed. That's worse.

**COUNCILMAN HARRIS**
Please — I didn't know it was your contract — I thought—

**TONY**
You thought you could skim off the top and nobody would notice. You're right. Nobody would have. I run a sanitation company, not the FBI.

**COUNCILMAN HARRIS**
Then what — what do you want?

**TONY**
I want you to understand something. When you take from me, you're not taking from a corporation. You're taking from people who work for me. Families. Guys who show up every day and do their jobs and expect me to protect them. And you made me look weak in front of my people.

**COUNCILMAN HARRIS**
I'll pay it back —

**TONY**
You'll do more than that. You're going to sign the contract back over to us. Full terms. And you're going to tell your friends at city hall that if they ever have a "problem" with Agentino Sanitation again, they should come directly to me.

**COUNCILMAN HARRIS**
And then?

**TONY**
And then we go back to how things were. Business as usual.

Tony steps back. Nods to someone off-screen.

The chair rolls forward. Into the darkness.

We hear a splash. Then screaming. Then silence.

---

**INT. DR. RUSSO'S OFFICE - DAY (CONTINUOUS)**

**DR. RUSSO**
Tony? You drifted.

**TONY**
(coming back)
Sorry. Long night.

**DR. RUSSO**
You mentioned "adjustment periods" twice now. Is that something you're dealing with personally? Feeling like you need time to adjust to... things?

**TONY**
Everyone needs adjustment time. Even the people who don't seem like they need it.

**DR. RUSSO**
What do you mean?

**TONY**
(choosing words carefully)
There's this guy at work. Sal. He's been with me a long time. Good worker. Loyal. But he gets... attached. To things. He doesn't like it when we have to... retrain someone.

**DR. RUSSO**
Retrain.

**TONY**
It's a workplace term.

**DR. RUSSO**
(smiling slightly)
You have very specific metaphors, Tony.

**TONY**
I was an English major.

**DR. RUSSO**
I didn't know that.

**TONY**
There's a lot you don't know about me, Doctor.

(beat)

I mean that as a good thing.

---

### CUTAWAY

**INT. AGENTINO SANITATION - BACK ROOM - NIGHT**

Sal (late 40s, big, dumb-loyal, a face that doesn't hide anything) stands in front of a tank. A big tank. Filled with water. And lobsters. A lot of them.

He's holding a clipboard. Speaking to the lobsters like they're a staff meeting.

**SAL**
Alright. Listen up. We got three new arrivals today. This is Frank. Frank's from Maine. Good stock. Strong claws — well, no, no claws, but strong spirit.

(to Frank)

You're gonna do great, buddy.

Tony walks in.

**TONY**
Sal.

**SAL**
Boss. These are the new guys. Frank, Carmine, and — what do you want to name this one?

**TONY**
I don't name them, Sal.

**SAL**
Right. Right. Business. I understand.

(beat)

Can we not use Frank this week? He just got here. He hasn't even settled in. It feels —

**TONY**
Sal.

**SAL**
I know. I know. It's just — he's got this look, Tony. This look like he trusts you.

Tony looks at Frank the lobster. Frank, of course, just sits there. Clawless.

**TONY**
(getting in the tank room)
We don't have a choice. Harris is meeting with the city council tomorrow. If he walks in there with any doubt in his mind, we lose the contract.

**SAL**
(defeated)
Yeah. Okay. I just — I'll go get the harness.

**TONY**
And Sal?

**SAL**
Yeah?

**TONY**
Name the other two whatever you want. But don't name Frank.

**SAL**
Why not?

**TONY**
(long pause)
Because he's the one they remember.

---

### COLD CLOSE

**INT. DR. RUSSO'S OFFICE - DAY**

**DR. RUSSO**
Tony, can I ask you something?

**TONY**
Sure.

**DR. RUSSO**
Why do you keep talking about "cleaning things up"?

**TONY**
Excuse me?

**DR. RUSSO**
In our last three sessions, you've used phrases like "file room," "correction," "maintenance," "process." You run a waste management company. I looked it up. But you talk about your job like it's... something else.

**TONY**
I'm in sanitation. We clean things up. It's literally the job.

**DR. RUSSO**
(leaning forward)
But you said "I made a man scream last Tuesday." In the context of a missing invoice. Do you know how that sounds?

Tony is quiet for a long time.

The camera lingers on his face.

**TONY**
(quietly)
Because the mess is always there, Doctor. You just move it around.

**DR. RUSSO**
I don't—

**TONY**
(end of session, standing)
Same time next week?

He leaves. She's left with her notebook, and a look on her face like she's just glimpsed something she can't quite name.

---

**EXT. AGENTINO SANITATION - NIGHT**

Tony walks out to his Cadillac Escalade. Black. Spotless. He gets in.

Sits there for a moment.

His phone buzzes. A text from Sal: "Frank says hi."

Tony looks at the text. Doesn't respond.

Starts the car.

Pulls out.

---

**INT. TONNY'S CADILLAC - CONTINUOUS**

The radio plays something old. Italian. Tony stares at the road.

His arm is sore. He rubs it absently.

On the dashboard: a photo of a wedding. Tony at a wedding. Crying. Happy.

The camera pulls back.

The Cadillac disappears down the road.

**TITLE CARD:**

*"The mess is always there. You just move it around."*

**TONY AGENTINO**
**Episode 1: The Claws Are Open**

🦞

### END OF EPISODE 1

## Notes for Production

- **Cold open:** Therapy session establishes the format — Tony speaks in metaphors, Dr. Russo is suspicious but charmed
- **Cutaway structure:** Two per episode minimum — one showing what actually happened, one showing the "workplace" (Sal + the tank)
- **Runtime:** ~4 minutes — tight, no fat
- **Tone:** Funny in the therapy room, dark in the cutaways, devastating in the silences
- **The tank:** Never show the full thing in Episode 1. Just hints. The glow. The hum. Frank.
- **Sal's attachment:** This is the emotional throughline — he's the audience's conscience, and it destroys him
- **Dr. Russo:** She thinks she's helping. She doesn't realize she's being studied.
- **End of episode:** Tony alone. The car. The arm. The wedding photo. He's the monster in the suit, and he knows it.
