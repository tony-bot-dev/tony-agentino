# Tony Agentino — Season 1 Episode Guide

**Format:** 3-5 min YouTube shorts  
**Premise:** Each episode is a therapy session (Tony narrates) with cutaways to what actually happened

---

## Episode 1: "The Claws Are Open"
**Status:** Pitch defines this — councilman skimming from contract

Tony in therapy describes "a problem with city paperwork." Dr. Russo asks why he keeps saying "paperwork" like it tastes bad. Cutaway: Sal names the new lobsters Frank, Carmine, and "that one, we don't talk about."

---

## Episode 2: "Frank's First Day"

A rival waste company moves into Tony's territory. Not mob — just some guy from Queens with a fleet of trucks and no idea what he's stepping into. Tony has to "handle" it without escalating. Sal is devastated when Frank gets called up for tank duty. The rival gets the full experience, calls his wife from the hospital, swears he'll "stay in his lane." Tony pays for the medical bills. Sal doesn't name any more lobsters for two weeks.

**Theme:** Mercy as a weapon, and who deserves it

---

## Episode 3: "The Adjustment Period"

Dr. Russo notices Tony's metaphors have gotten "military." He talks about "reconnaissance" and "collateral." Cutaway: Tony's guys are surveilling a bookkeeper who skimmed from the wrong envelope. The bookkeeper's a single mom. Tony tells Marco to "find another way." Marco beats the shit out of her car with a baseball bat. It's not enough. She's still talking.

Tony visits the tank alone at 3am. Talks to Frank.

**Theme:** The difference between what Tony orders and what happens

---

## Episode 4: "Sal's Day Off"

It's Sal's birthday. He wants the day off to go to Atlantic City. Tony says go, have fun, we got this. Of course something goes wrong — a guys' weekend turns into a hostage situation (not Tony's guys, some other outfit). Sal gets caught in the crossfire. Tony has to extract him. The tank gets used — not on the other guys, on one of Sal's own guys who set them up. Sal comes back different. Doesn't talk about Atlantic City again.

**Theme:** Loyalty costs

---

## Episode 5: "The Glow"

The Tank starts doing something weird. The water's different. The lobsters are calmer. A weird blue glow comes from somewhere in the basement nobody knew about. Sal's convinced it's haunted. Tony doesn't believe in that stuff — but he stops sleeping. Dr. Russo asks why he keeps rubbing his arms like they're sore. He says he "went swimming."

Cutaway reveals: The Tank isn't just a tank. There's something under it. Old. The previous owner left a basement under the basement. Tony found it years ago. Never told anyone.

**Theme:** The past you bury doesn't stay buried

---

## Episode 6: "Marco's Choice"

Marco's brother gets arrested. Federal. Wants to flip. Wants protection. Tony can't give him protection — not without exposing everything. Marco asks Tony to "make the problem go away." Tony says no. Marco does it himself. Brings the brother to the tank. The brother talks for six hours. Marco listens. Then pulls him out.

Next therapy session: Tony describes "a student who exceeded the curriculum." Dr. Russo says "that sounds like a tragedy." Tony says "that sounds like a promotion."

**Theme:** The mirror fights back

---

## Episode 7: "What Mom Knows"

Donna Agentino has a stroke. Minor. She's in the hospital. While Tony's sitting by her bed, she wakes up, looks at him, and says: "I know what the tank is. I always knew. Your father didn't die in '98. He died in that basement."

Tony's whole world shifts. He goes to the tank. Sits on the edge. The lobsters are calm. Frank climbs up and rests on his hand.

Dr. Russo next session: Tony describes "family business." She's never seen him this quiet. She pushes. He says "I think someone in my family was braver than I am."

**Theme:** The code is older than you

---

## Episode 8: "The Line"

Finale. The feds are circling. Tony has to decide: go down clean, or burn it all down. He could flip — give up everything, everyone. But that breaks the code. He could run — but then Marco runs the business, and Marco's already shown what he'll do.

Tony calls a meeting. Everyone's there. Sal. Marco. The guys. He lays out the plan: everyone's getting out. Papers are forged. Accounts are split. It's over.

Then he goes to the basement. Stands over the tank. The lobsters are calm. They always are.

He doesn't get in. But he doesn't walk away either. He just stands there. Camera doesn't cut away.

End of season.

**Theme:** The line you don't cross — and what happens when you can't see it anymore

---

## B-Roll / Running Gags

- Sal naming lobsters (Frank, Carmine, "that one")
- Tony crying at wedding commercials (cut to brutal thing immediately after)
- Dr. Russo's office plants — she has too many, they're dying
- The hum of the tank, always there
- Tony's suits, always perfect, never mentioned

---

## Notes

- **No cliffhangers** — each episode has a complete story
- **Therapy sessions frame everything** — Tony's metaphors are the hook
- **Tank = honesty** — it's where Tony is most himself
- **No graphic violence** — implication over action
- **The lobsters are sympathetic** — they're victims too, in their own way
