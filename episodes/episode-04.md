# Tony Agentino — Episode 4
## "Sal's Day Off"

**Length:** ~4 minutes  
**Format:** Cold open (Tony in therapy) → Cutaway → Therapy → Cutaway → Therapy → Cutaway → Cold close

---

### COLD OPEN

**INT. DR. RUSSO'S OFFICE - DAY**

Tony sits in the chair. He looks... lighter. There's something different about him. Not good. Just different.

**DR. RUSSO**
You seem different today.

**TONY**
Different how?

**DR. RUSSO**
I don't know. More... present. Like you're actually here.

**TONY**
I had a birthday at work.

**DR. RUSSO**
A birthday?

**TONY**
One of my guys. Sal. He's been with me a long time. Since the beginning. He wanted the day off. Said he had plans.

**DR. RUSSO**
Did he have plans?

**TONY**
(long pause)
He had ideas.

---

### CUTAWAY

**INT. AGENTINO SANITATION - DAY - ONE WEEK EARLIER**

The office is decorated. Badly. Streamers everywhere. A cake that says "HAPPY BIRTHDAY SAL" in frosting that's already sliding off.

Sal stands in the middle of it. Big guy. Bigger smile.

**SAL**
This is the best thing that's ever happened to me.

**TONY**
(deadpan)
I've seen better.

**SAL**
You didn't have to do all this.

**TONY**
You didn't give me a choice. You mentioned it. In detail. For three weeks.

**SAL**
Yeah. Well. I was thinking —

**TONY**
Dangerous.

**SAL**
— I was thinking Atlantic City. Just for the day. You know. The tables. The comps. The —

**TONY**
The something you don't tell me about.

**SAL**
(catching himself)
Right. Right. Just guys stuff. Male bonding. Nothing weird.

Tony looks at him. Doesn't believe a word.

**TONY**
Go. Have fun. We got things covered here.

**SAL**
You sure?

**TONY**
I'm sure.

**SAL**
(big hug)
You're the best boss I've ever had. And I've had —

**TONY**
Go.

Sal leaves. Waves. Disappears.

Tony turns to Marco, who's been standing in the corner.

**TONY**
He's going to get himself killed.

**MARCO**
He's going to get himself arrested.

**TONY**
Same thing.

---

### CUTAWAY

**INT. DR. RUSSO'S OFFICE - DAY**

**DR. RUSSO**
So he went? To Atlantic City?

**TONY**
He went.

**DR. RUSSO**
And?

**TONY**
And something happened.

**DR. RUSSO**
Something bad?

**TONY**
(choosing words)
Something that wasn't supposed to happen.

**DR. RUSSO**
At the casino?

**TONY**
At a casino. Just not the one Sal was going to.

---

### CUTAWAY

**INT. ATLANTIC CITY CASINO - NIGHT**

A different casino. Flashy. Loud. The kind of place that promises everything and delivers nothing.

Sal is there. He's winning. At the blackjack table. He's on a streak.

**SAL**
Hot damn! Again! Who's the man! Who's the MAN!

Other players look at him. Some annoyed. Some amused.

A man — BIG FRANKIE (50s, connected, the kind of guy who wears a gold chain and pretends it's not a statement) — walks up behind Sal.

**BIG FRANKIE**
Easy there, big guy.

**SAL**
(turning)
Who the hell are you?

**BIG FRANKIE**
I'm the guy whose table you're winning at.

**SAL**
It's a free country.

**BIG FRANKIE**
It's my casino.

Sal looks around. Realizes something's wrong.

**SAL**
(backing up)
I was just leaving.

**BIG FRANKIE**
Not so fast.

Two guys appear. Big guys. Bouncers.

**BIG FRANKIE**
You owe me twelve thousand dollars.

**SAL**
I just won twelve thousand dollars!

**BIG FRANKIE**
And now you owe me more.

**SAL**
(panicking)
I don't have that kind of money!

**BIG FRANKIE**
Then you better call someone who does.

Sal takes out his phone. Dialing.

---

### CUTAWAY

**INT. AGENTINO SANITATION - NIGHT**

Tony's at his desk. It's late. He looks at his phone. Sees Sal's name.

**TONY**
(answering)
Sal. You better not be calling me from a jail cell.

**SAL** (V.O.)
Boss. I need help.

**TONY**
What did you do?

**SAL** (V.O.)
I may have — I may have gotten into a thing. At a casino. With some guys. And they may have taken me somewhere. And they may have pointed guns at me.

**TONY**
Sal.

**SAL** (V.O.)
Yeah?

**TONY**
Where are you?

**SAL** (V.O.)
I don't know. Some warehouse. By the water. There's boats outside.

**TONY**
Stay on the phone.

---

### CUTAWAY

**INT. WAREHOUSE - NIGHT**

Sal is duct-taped to a chair. Same room as before. But this time it's different. The tank is here. In the corner. Big Frankie's guys dragged it. This is personal now.

Big Frankie stands over Sal.

**BIG FRANKIE**
You got five minutes, big guy. Then I'm filling that tank with something other than water.

**SAL**
(terrified)
You don't know who you're messing with.

**BIG FRANKIE**
I know exactly who I'm messing with. Some waste management guy from Jersey. You think I'm scared of that?

A door bursts open.

Tony walks in. Marco behind him. Both armed.

**TONY**
I don't think you're scared. I think you're about to be.

**BIG FRANKIE**
Tony Agentino. Should have known.

**TONY**
You should have.

**BIG FRANKIE**
This your guy?

**TONY**
That's my guy.

**BIG FRANKIE**
He owes me money.

**TONY**
He owes me more.

Tony walks up to Big Frankie. Face to face.

**TONY**
Let him go. Now. And I'll forget this happened.

**BIG FRANKIE**
Or?

**TONY**
Or I'll show you what Agentino Sanitation actually does.

A standoff. Guns drawn. Tension fills the room.

**BIG FRANKIE**
(backing down)
Fine. Take him. But we're not done.

**TONY**
We're done when I say we're done.

Sal is cut loose. Marco pulls him out.

Tony stays.

**TONY**
(to Big Frankie)
You picked the wrong guy to mess with.

**BIG FRANKIE**
This isn't over.

**TONY**
It never is.

---

### CUTAWAY

**INT. TANK ROOM - BACK AT HOME - NIGHT**

The tank. Sal's tank. The one in the basement. It's different now. Quieter.

Sal stands in front of it. Tony beside him.

**SAL**
I thought I was dead.

**TONY**
You almost were.

**SAL**
Those guys — they had guns. Real guns. And they were going to —

**TONY**
I know.

**SAL**
What were you going to do? If I hadn't gotten out?

Tony looks at the tank. The water. The hum.

**TONY**
Something I don't do anymore.

**SAL**
What?

**TONY**
Something that ends permanent.

Sal looks at Tony. Then at the tank.

**SAL**
That thing in the warehouse. The one they brought.

**TONY**
That's not ours. That's theirs. I just... borrowed it.

**SAL**
For what?

**TONY**
For when the guns aren't enough.

A long silence.

**SAL**
Tony.

**TONY**
Yeah?

**SAL**
Don't tell me. I don't want to know.

**TONY**
(small smile)
Wasn't going to.

---

### CUTAWAY

**INT. DR. RUSSO'S OFFICE - DAY**

**DR. RUSSO**
So your guy. Sal. He got into trouble?

**TONY**
He got into trouble. Someone else's trouble. Not his.

**DR. RUSSO**
But you helped him.

**TONY**
That's what I do.

**DR. RUSSO**
And everything worked out?

**TONY**
(pause)
Everything worked out.

**DR. RUSSO**
But?

**TONY**
But nothing's free. You know that.

**DR. RUSSO**
What did it cost?

**TONY**
(standing)
A decision. I had to make a decision I haven't made in a long time.

**DR. RUSSO**
What kind of decision?

**TONY**
The kind where someone else pays.

(beat)

That's the only kind that matters.

**DR. RUSSO**
Tony—

**TONY**
(already standing)
Same time next week.

---

### CUTAWAY

**INT. TANK ROOM - NIGHT**

Tony stands alone. Late. 3am again.

He's looking at the tank. But not Frank. Someone else.

A lobster. Bigger. Older. Sitting in the corner. Not moving.

**TONY**
(quietly)
I was going to use you tonight. You know that.

The lobster doesn't respond.

**TONY**
Would have been easy. One call. One trip. And everything would have stayed —

He stops. Looks at his hands.

**TONY**
I'm tired of things staying the same.

The glow pulses. This time it's red. Just for a second.

Then it's gone.

---

### COLD CLOSE

**INT. DR. RUSSO'S OFFICE - DAY - NEXT WEEK**

The office is quiet. Tony's not there yet.

Dr. Russo looks at her notebook. Flips through the pages.

Her phone buzzes. Text from Sal: "Thanks for everything. You're a good doctor."

She looks at the text. Confusion.

Then Tony walks in. Sits down.

**DR. RUSSO**
That was weird.

**TONY**
What?

**DR. RUSSO**
One of your guys just texted me. Said thank you.

**TONY**
(pause)
That was Sal.

**DR. RUSSO**
He seemed... different.

**TONY**
He is different.

**DR. RUSSO**
How?

**TONY**
(looking at her)
He went through something. Came out the other side.

**DR. RUSSO**
And did he come out better or worse?

**TONY**
(long pause)
That's not how it works.

**DR. RUSSO**
How does it work?

**TONY**
You come out different. That's the only promise anyone can make.

He leans back. Looks at the ceiling.

**TONY**
Next week I want to talk about my father.

**DR. RUSSO**
(surprised)
Your father? You've never mentioned him.

**TONY**
There's a lot I haven't mentioned.

(beat)

But it's time.

---

**EXT. AGENTINO SANITATION - NIGHT**

Tony walks out. Gets in his Cadillac.

Sits there.

His phone buzzes. Text from Sal: "I don't talk about Atlantic City anymore."

Tony looks at the text. Types back: "Good."

He puts the phone down. Rubs his arm.

Looks at the wedding photo.

Pulls out.

---

**TITLE CARD:**

*"The mess is always there. You just move it around."*

**TONY AGENTINO**
**Episode 4: Sal's Day Off**

## Notes for Production

- **Sal's trauma:** He's changed. He doesn't talk about Atlantic City. He doesn't name new lobsters. He's quieter.
- **Tony almost broke:** He was going to use the tank. On Big Frankie. The old way. He didn't. But it cost him something.
- **The glow:** Red this time. Something's shifting.
- **Tony's father:** Dropped for Episode 7. He's opening up. That's dangerous.
- **Dr. Russo:** She got a text from Sal. That's weird. That's a boundary crossing. She's in deeper than she knows.
- **Marco:** We don't see much of him this episode, but his presence is felt. He's becoming Tony's problem.
- **Tease for Episode 5:** "The Glow" — the tank starts doing something weird. The water's different. The basement under the basement. Sal's convinced it's haunted. Tony doesn't sleep.
- **The older lobster:** There's one in the tank that Tony talks to. Bigger. Older. Not Frank. Something else.
