# Tony Agentino — Episode 3
## "The Adjustment Period"

**Length:** ~4 minutes  
**Format:** Cold open (Tony in therapy) → Cutaway → Therapy → Cutaway → Therapy → Cold close

---

### COLD OPEN

**INT. DR. RUSSO'S OFFICE - DAY**

Tony sits in the chair. He looks tired. Not the kind of tired sleep fixes.

**DR. RUSSO**
You look different this week.

**TONY**
Different how?

**DR. RUSSO**
I don't know. Lighter, maybe. But in a bad way. Like something's been removed.

**TONY**
(starting)
I've been — I've been doing some cleaning. At work. You know how it is. Things pile up. You have to decide what stays and what goes.

**DR. RUSSO**
That's very minimalist of you.

**TONY**
(small smile)
I'm full of surprises.

---

### CUTAWAY

**INT. AGENTINO SANITATION - DAY**

MARCO (late 20s, sharp, dangerous, the kind of smart that makes you uncomfortable) stands in Tony's office.

**MARCO**
She's a bookkeeper. Works for one of our vendors. She's been skimming from the invoices for about eight months.

**TONY**
Eight months?

**MARCO**
Small amounts. $500 here, $1,000 there. But it adds up.

**TONY**
How much total?

**MARCO**
About eighteen grand.

Tony leans back. Thinks.

**TONY**
She's doing this alone?

**MARCO**
Looks like it. Single mom. Two kids. House in Linden.

**TONY**
(sighing)
Of course it's a house in Linden.

**MARCO**
What do you want me to do?

**TONY**
(long pause)
What do you mean, what do you want me to do?

**MARCO**
Usually you have a process. File correction. Personnel review. The... maintenance.

**TONY**
(standing, walking to window)
This one doesn't fit the process.

**MARCO**
Because she's a woman?

**TONY**
Because she's got kids. Two of them. And she's not — she's not one of us. She's just some woman who saw an opportunity and took it.

**MARCO**
That's still theft.

**TONY**
I know it's theft. I'm not saying she's innocent. I'm saying she's not ours.

**MARCO**
So what do you want me to do?

**TONY**
(turning)
I want you to find another way. Not the tank. Not anything that ends with her not coming home. Scare her. Make her understand. But she walks away.

**MARCO**
(pause)
That's not really my style.

**TONY**
It's not about your style. It's about what I can live with.

**MARCO**
And if she talks?

**TONY**
She won't.

**MARCO**
How do you know?

**TONY**
Because you'll make sure she doesn't.

Marco nods. Leaves.

Tony turns back to the window.

---

### CUTAWAY

**EXT. LINDEN, NJ - NIGHT**

A modest house. A sedan in the driveway. A minivan. Kids' bikes on the porch.

Marco's car pulls up. He gets out. He's holding a baseball bat.

He walks to the minivan. Starts hitting it.

Bam. Bam. Bam.

The windows shatter. The alarms go off. The whole neighborhood lights up.

A woman — DEBBIE (early 30s, terrified, clutching her phone — she was just about to call the police) — runs out of the house.

**DEBBIE**
STOP! WHAT ARE YOU — WHO ARE YOU?!

Marco stops. Looks at her.

**MARCO**
You know what you did. You know who you took from. You have 48 hours to put it back. All of it. Plus interest.

**DEBBIE**
I don't — I don't understand —

**MARCO**
(explaining slowly)
Eighteen thousand dollars. Plus two thousand for the van. You have 48 hours. If I see a police report, if I hear a word, if I so much as see you look at one of my people wrong — next time I'm not hitting the van.

He drops the bat. Walks to his car. Drives away.

Debbie stands there. Shaking.

---

### CUTAWAY

**INT. DR. RUSSO'S OFFICE - DAY**

**DR. RUSSO**
You seem agitated.

**TONY**
Just thinking about someone at work. I gave them a task. A simple task. Find another way. That's what I said. Find another way.

**DR. RUSSO**
And they didn't?

**TONY**
They did. It just — it wasn't the way I meant.

**DR. RUSSO**
What way did they mean?

**TONY**
(choosing words)
They went... physical. On something that wasn't the actual problem.

**DR. RUSSO**
That sounds like a failure to communicate.

**TONY**
That's one way to put it.

**DR. RUSSO**
Did it work?

**TONY**
The message was received.

**DR. RUSSO**
But?

**TONY**
But now I'm wondering if I sent the right message.

**DR. RUSSO**
Maybe you should clarify.

**TONY**
Maybe I should.

(beat)

Can I ask you something?

**DR. RUSSO**
Of course.

**TONY**
If you tell someone to do something — something simple, something clear — and they do it, but they do it wrong... whose fault is it?

**DR. RUSSO**
That's a tricky question.

**TONY**
I didn't ask for easy.

**DR. RUSSO**
(thinking)
I think — I think intent matters. If you gave a clear instruction and they followed it in spirit, that's different than if they—

**TONY**
I told them to find another way. Not the usual way. A different way. Something that didn't end bad.

**DR. RUSSO**
And they chose something that did end bad.

**TONY**
They chose something that ended... gray. There's no coming back from gray.

**DR. RUSSO**
Is this about work or something else?

**TONY**
(standing)
It's always about work, Doctor.

---

### CUTAWAY

**INT. TANK ROOM - NIGHT**

Tony stands in front of the tank. It's late. 3am. The hum fills everything.

Frank is there. Always there.

**TONY**
(quietly)
I told him another way. That's what I said. Another way. Not this. Not —

He puts his hand on the glass.

**TONY**
I keep thinking I can draw a line. One more time. One more time and then I stop. But the line keeps moving. You know?

Frank doesn't respond.

**TONY**
She has kids, Frank. Two of them. And now she's going to wake up every night thinking someone's coming for her. That's not — that's not what I wanted.

The glow pulses. Faint blue.

**TONY**
I wanted her to be scared. Just scared. Not destroyed. There's a difference.

He stands there. For a long time.

**TONY**
(finally)
Maybe there isn't.

---

### COLD CLOSE

**INT. DR. RUSSO'S OFFICE - DAY - NEXT SESSION**

**DR. RUSSO**
Tony. You missed last Tuesday.

**TONY**
I had something come up.

**DR. RUSSO**
You never miss.

**TONY**
I had to handle something. At work.

**DR. RUSSO**
The same work that has you using words like "physical" and "gray"?

**TONY**
(pause)
Something like that.

**DR. RUSSO**
(leaning forward)
I've been thinking about our conversation. About the line. About whose fault it is when someone follows instructions wrong.

**TONY**
And?

**DR. RUSSO**
I think — I think you're being too hard on yourself. You gave someone a task. They did it their way. That's not on you.

**TONY**
(small laugh)
That's very generous of you.

**DR. RUSSO**
I'm just saying. You can't control everything.

**TONY**
(no laugh now)
No. You can't.

He stands. Puts on his jacket.

**TONY**
Same time next week?

**DR. RUSSO**
Will you be here?

**TONY**
I'll be here.

He leaves.

She looks at her notebook. Writes something: "Something's wrong. Not his job. Him."

---

**EXT. AGENTINO SANITATION - NIGHT**

Tony walks out. Gets in his Cadillac.

Sits there.

Takes out his phone. Texts Marco: "We need to talk."

Then: "About the line."

He puts the phone down. Rubs his arm.

Looks at the wedding photo.

Pulls out.

## Notes for Production

- **Theme:** The difference between what Tony orders and what happens. He said "another way" and Marco heard "make sure she doesn't talk."
- **Tony's unraveling:** He's starting to realize the line keeps moving. The gray. The in-between.
- **Debbie:** She pays back the money. She's fine. But she's not okay. The fear is permanent.
- **Frank scene:** 3am, Tony alone, talking to the lobster. This is where he's most honest.
- **Dr. Russo:** She's starting to piece it together. "Something's wrong. Not his job. Him."
- **Marco:** He's becoming the problem Tony created. He's too smart, too willing, too loyal.
- **Tease for Episode 4:** Sal's birthday is coming up. He's going to Atlantic City. Something goes wrong.
