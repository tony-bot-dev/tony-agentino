# Tony Agentino — Episode 7
## "What Mom Knows"

**Length:** ~4 minutes  
**Format:** Cold open (hospital) → Cutaway (memory) → Hospital → Cutaway → Hospital → Cold close

---

### COLD OPEN

**INT. HOSPITAL ROOM - DAY**

White walls. Beeping machines. The smell of antiseptic.

Tony sits in a chair by the bed. His mother — DONNA AGENTINO (70s, tiny, terrifying, even in a hospital gown) — lies in the bed. Eyes closed.

A doctor stands in the doorway. Tony steps out.

**DOCTOR**
It was a minor stroke. She's going to be fine. But she needs rest.

**TONY**
How long?

**DOCTOR**
A few days. Maybe a week. We'll monitor her.

**TONY**
Thank you.

The doctor leaves.

Tony goes back to the room. Sits by his mother's bed.

Takes her hand.

**TONY**
(whispering)
I'm here, Ma.

Her eyes open. Slowly. Focuses on him.

**DONNA**
(weak, but sharp)
You look like shit.

**TONY**
(small smile)
You look worse.

**DONNA**
Liar.

A long moment. She studies his face.

**DONNA**
Something's wrong.

**TONY**
Nothing's wrong.

**DONNA**
You're a terrible liar. You always have been. Your father was too.

She says it casually. Like it's nothing.

Tony freezes.

**TONY**
What?

**DONNA**
Your father. He was a terrible liar too. Couldn't hide anything from me. Not ever.

**TONY**
(controlled)
What are you talking about?

**DONNA**
(eyes closing)
I know about the tank, Anthony. I've always known.

---

### CUTAWAY

**INT. TONNY'S APARTMENT - FLASHBACK - NIGHT**

A younger Tony (late 20s, not yet the man we know) stands in the kitchen. His father — SALVATORE AGENTINO SR. (60s, big, old-school, the kind of man who doesn't talk about things) — sits at the table.

**SALVATORE**
You understand what I'm telling you?

**TONY**
I understand.

**SALVATORE**
The tank is older than this family. Your grandfather brought it over. From the old country. From a place where men made men understand things.

**TONY**
I know.

**SALVATORE**
You don't know anything. You're a kid. But one day you'll be the man. And you'll have to make choices I couldn't make.

**TONY**
What choices?

**SALVATORE**
(standing)
The choice of when to stop.

**TONY**
Stop what?

**SALVATORE**
(looking at him)
Everything.

He leaves.

Young Tony stands alone in the kitchen.

---

### CUTAWAY

**INT. HOSPITAL ROOM - DAY**

**TONY**
(controlled)
Ma. What are you saying?

**DONNA**
I'm saying — I'm saying I knew. All of it. The tank. What you do. What your father did. What his father did.

**TONY**
How?

**DONNA**
(wry smile)
I wasn't born yesterday, Anthony.

**TONY**
You never — you never said anything.

**DONNA**
Why would I? You were — you were good at it. Better than your father. Better than your grandfather. You had a system. You had rules.

**TONY**
(quietly)
I have rules.

**DONNA**
I know you do. That's what I mean. Your father didn't have rules. That's why he's dead.

Tony goes very still.

**TONY**
What?

**DONNA**
(eyes opening, focusing on him)
Your father didn't die in '98. He died in the basement. In the tank. He went in. And he never came out.

---

### CUTAWAY

**INT. SECRET BASEMENT - FLASHBACK - NIGHT**

Salvatore Agentino stands in front of the pool. The glow. The hum. The lobsters.

He's not restrained. He's not duct-taped. He's just standing there.

Young Tony (early 30s now, the man we know) stands behind him.

**SALVATORE**
I told you. I told you everything. I gave you the business. The contacts. The code. What more do you want?

**TONY**
I want you to stop.

**SALVATORE**
Stop what?

**TONY**
Talking. To the feds. To the lawyers. To everyone who's listening.

**SALVATORE**
I'm not talking to anyone!

**TONY**
You're talking to me. Right now. In this room. That's enough.

**SALVATORE**
(spinning around)
What are you going to do? Huh? Put me in the tank? I'm your father!

**TONY**
You're a liability.

**SALVATORE**
I'm your blood!

**TONY**
Blood doesn't mean anything in this family. You taught me that.

They stand there. Father and son. The glow pulses between them.

**SALVATORE**
(quieter)
Then do it. Put me in. You know you want to.

**TONY**
I don't want to.

**SALVATORE**
But you will. Because that's what we do. That's what this family does. We put our problems in the water and we wait for them to come out different.

**TONY**
I won't put you in.

**SALVATORE**
Then what?

**TONY**
I'll go in with you.

Salvatore looks at his son.

**SALVATORE**
What?

**TONY**
We'll go in together. Father and son. And we'll figure it out. Like we always have.

**SALVATORE**
(touched, then)
This is crazy.

**TONY**
This is family.

They step into the pool together.

The glow intensifies.

The hum fills everything.

---

### CUTAWAY

**INT. HOSPITAL ROOM - DAY**

**TONY**
(voice breaking)
Ma. Stop. Please.

**DONNA**
(sharper now)
You think I don't know what happened? Your father came home that night. Wet. Shaking. He didn't talk for three days. And then he told me: "Donna, I went into the water with my son. And something looked back."

**TONY**
That's — that's not—

**DONNA**
And then, a week later, he died. "Industrial accident." That's what they said. That's what we told everyone.

**TONY**
He had a heart attack.

**DONNA**
He had a heart attack because he saw something in that basement that he couldn't live with. And neither will you.

**TONY**
Ma.

**DONNA**
(grabbing his hand)
Listen to me, Anthony. Your father was braver than you. He went into the water knowing what it would do. And he came out broken. But he came out. You — you're still in there. You've been in there since that night. And you haven't come out yet.

---

### CUTAWAY

**INT. TANK ROOM - NIGHT**

Tony stands in front of the tank. The glow is blue. The hum fills everything.

Frank is there. Tony the lobster is there. All of them.

He puts his hand on the glass.

**TONY**
(quietly)
I haven't left. Not since that night. I haven't left.

The lobsters move. Frank climbs up. Rests his clawless limb on the glass. Like he's listening.

**TONY**
My father came in here. With me. And something looked back. And he couldn't live with it.

Frank doesn't respond.

**TONY**
I'm living with it. Every day. That's the difference between us. I can live with it.

The glow pulses. This time it's warm. Almost gentle.

**TONY**
Maybe that's the curse. Maybe that's the gift. I don't know anymore.

---

### CUTAWAY

**INT. HOSPITAL ROOM - DAY**

**DONNA**
(weaker now)
I need you to promise me something.

**TONY**
Anything.

**DONNA**
When it's your time — when you can't do this anymore — don't go to the water. Don't go to the basement. Don't go looking for answers that aren't there.

**TONY**
I don't understand.

**DONNA**
You do. You've always understood more than you say. That's your problem. That's your gift. But the tank — the tank doesn't give answers. It just takes time.

**TONY**
(tears now)
I'll think about it.

**DONNA**
(almost asleep)
Think about it now. While you still can.

She closes her eyes.

Tony sits there. Holding her hand.

---

### COLD CLOSE

**INT. DR. RUSSO'S OFFICE - DAY**

Tony sits in the chair. He looks like he's been through a war.

**DR. RUSSO**
Tony. You missed two sessions.

**TONY**
My mother was in the hospital.

**DR. RUSSO**
Is she okay?

**TONY**
She's going to be fine.

**DR. RUSSO**
That's good. That's — that's really good.

**TONY**
She told me something. About my father.

**DR. RUSSO**
Your father?

**TONY**
She told me — she told me he was braver than me.

**DR. RUSSO**
Braver how?

**TONY**
He went into the water. With me. And he saw something. And he couldn't live with what he saw.

**DR. RUSSO**
What did he see?

**TONY**
(quietly)
I think — I think I understand now.

**DR. RUSSO**
Understand what?

**TONY**
Why he died. Why I keep rubbing my arm. Why I can't sleep. Why — why I keep coming here.

**DR. RUSSO**
Tony—

**TONY**
Because I went into the water. With my father. And something looked back. And I'm still standing here. But I'm not the same person who went in.

She writes something down. Looks at him.

**DR. RUSSO**
Who were you before?

**TONY**
I was — I was someone who thought he was in control.

**DR. RUSSO**
And now?

**TONY**
Now I know I'm not.

(beat)

And that's the most terrifying thing I've ever admitted.

**DR. RUSSO**
Can I ask you something?

**TONY**
Yes.

**DR. RUSSO**
What happened to your father? In the water?

**TONY**
(slowly)
He became part of the tank.

**DR. RUSSO**
I don't—

**TONY**
The lobsters. They have a hierarchy. The oldest one — the one that's been there the longest — that's him. That's my father.

She stares at him.

**DR. RUSSO**
Tony. Are you — are you saying your father is literally—

**TONY**
I'm saying — I'm saying there's more to this than I understand. And I'm saying I need to stop.

**DR. RUSSO**
Stop what?

**TONY**
Everything.

He stands. Puts on his jacket.

**DR. RUSSO**
Tony. Wait.

**TONY**
I have to think. I have to — I have to figure out who I am. Now that I know what I've done.

**DR. RUSSO**
Will I see you next week?

**TONY**
(pausing at the door)
I don't know.

He leaves.

She stares at the door.

Then at her notebook.

She writes: "He's breaking. The water. The father. Something's coming."

---

**EXT. HOSPITAL - DAY**

Tony walks out. Stops on the sidewalk.

Takes out his phone. Texts Sal: "I'm done. This is it. We're shutting it down."

Then: "Not the tank. The whole thing."

Then: "Thank you. For everything."

He puts the phone away.

Gets in the Cadillac.

Sits there.

Rubs his arm. Looks at the wedding photo.

Pulls out.

---

**TITLE CARD:**

*"The mess is always there. You just move it around."*

**TONY AGENTINO**
**Episode 7: What Mom Knows**

## Notes for Production

- **The revelation:** Donna tells Tony that his father died in the tank. Not literally — he had a heart attack after going in. But he's "part of the tank" now. The oldest lobster. The one that watches.
- **The flashback:** Young Tony goes into the pool with his father. Something looks back. That's when the arm started hurting. That's when everything changed.
- **Donna's warning:** "The tank doesn't give answers. It just takes time." And "When it's your time — don't go looking for answers."
- **Tony's decision:** He's done. He's shutting it down. Text to Sal: "We're shutting it down."
- **Dr. Russo:** She doesn't understand. But she's there. And she's starting to see Tony as something other than a patient.
- **The arm:** Still sore. Still no full explanation. But now we know: it started after he went into the water with his father.
- **Setup for Episode 8:** Tony stands over the tank. Everyone's getting out. The code. The family. Everything. He has to decide: go down clean, or burn it all down.
- **The ending:** Ambiguous. He drives away. But we don't know if he's really done. We don't know if the tank lets him go.
